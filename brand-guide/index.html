Foundation

Brand Principles

Six rules that guide every design decision — slides, web, docs, infographics, and everything in between.

visibility

Clarity First

If a design choice reduces legibility or comprehension, it's wrong. Every decision serves understanding.

light_mode

White as the Stage

Default to white backgrounds. Let typography and data do the work. Color is an accent, not the story.

filter_center_focus

One Focal Point

Every slide, screen, and page has one obvious hero message or visual. No competing for attention.

brush

Color as a Highlighter

Cool greys for structure. Brand colors for accents and CTAs only. Never use color as wallpaper.

grid_view

Consistent Across Channels

Same components and rules for docs, slides, web, infographics — internal and external, always.

accessibility_new

Accessible by Default

WCAG 2.1 AA is the floor, not the ceiling. Contrast, focus states, and semantics are non-negotiable.

Motion & Animation

Motion Principles

Animate with purpose. Every motion should add clarity or guide attention — never distract or delight at the expense of performance.

transition_fade

Fade & Slide Entrance

Key elements enter with a gentle fade combined with a short upward translate. Used for section reveals and card entrances.

opacity 0→1 + translateY 12px→0
touch_app

Hover Transform

Buttons and cards lift slightly on hover. Subtle, fast, and never more than 2px. Communicates interactivity without noise.

translateY -2px · 150ms ease
swap_horiz

Slide Transitions (Decks)

Simple fade or none. Never spinning, zooming, or bouncing transitions in presentation decks.

Fade only · No kinetic transitions
Always: Animate transform and opacity only — never width, height, top, or left. Respect prefers-reduced-motion on all web properties. Avoid looping animations on public-facing pages.
